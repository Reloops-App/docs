---
title: "Manage Project Reviewers and Share Links in Reloops"
sidebarTitle: "Reviewers & Links"
description: "Use the Reviewers and Links tabs on a project to track guest access, check which assets reviewers can see, and audit or clean up active share links."
---

Every project has two tabs that give you a clear picture of who has access to your assets and how. The **Reviewers** tab shows guest reviewers and the assets they have been assigned to. The **Links** tab shows every active asset share link in the project along with its expiry date. Together, these tabs let you stay on top of external access without having to open individual assets.

## Reviewers tab

The Reviewers tab lists each guest reviewer who has been given access to an asset in this project. For each entry you can see:

- The reviewer's name or email address
- The asset or assets they have been assigned to review

Use this tab to confirm that a reviewer has been set up correctly and has access to the right asset before you follow up with them.

## Links tab

The Links tab lists every active asset share link that has been created for assets in this project. For each link you can see the asset it points to and whether an expiration date is set. Use this tab to audit external access at a glance and to identify links that are still active after a review window has closed.

## When to check these tabs

<Accordion title="A reviewer says they cannot access an asset">
  Open the Reviewers tab and confirm the reviewer appears in the list and is assigned to the correct asset. If they are missing, go to the asset and share it again. If they appear but still cannot access it, check whether the share link has expired in the Links tab.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="A project has several external stakeholders">
  Use the Reviewers tab to get a consolidated view of everyone with guest access across the project. This is faster than opening each asset individually to check who has been invited.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="A review window is finished and old links need cleaning up">
  Open the Links tab and review which links are still active. Expire or revoke any links that should no longer be accessible now that the review cycle is complete. Keeping old links active increases the risk of unintended access to project assets.
</Accordion>
